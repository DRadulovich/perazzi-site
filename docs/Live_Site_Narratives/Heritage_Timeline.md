# Heritage & Legacy Narrative

## Page Thesis
- Celebrates the workshop's journey from Daniele Perazzi's teenage apprenticeship to modern Olympic dominance.
- Tone: archival, documentary, yet personal; copy invites readers to "walk the timeline like you are thumbing through a craftsman's notebook."

## Timeline Highlights
1. **1957–1964 – Origins**
   - Daniele opens the Gardone Val Trompia workshop, collaborates with Ivo Fabbri, crafts the prototype that wins Ennio Mattarelli's 1964 Olympic trap gold.
2. **1968–1976 – MX Era Emerges**
   - Launch of MX8 for Mexico City Olympics; introduction of removable trigger, modular barrels; Ithaca begins import to the U.S.
3. **1980s – Champions & Innovations**
   - DB81 (for Dan Bonillas), MX10, TM1/TMX/TM9 for American trap; timeline cards include quotes from Ray Stafford and Lones Wigger.
4. **1990s–2010s – Global Expansion**
   - MX2000 series, High Tech research begins, Perazzi Invitational established, Gardone atelier expanded, Kim Rhode/ Michael Diamond gold streaks.
5. **Today & Future**
   - Mauro and Roberta Perazzi steward the house; partnership with CSG ensures continuity; emphasis on limited annual output and bespoke future tech.

## Supporting Modules
- **Photo Essay** – alternating black-and-white workshop scenes with modern competition stills; captions highlight intangible craft lessons.
- **Oral Histories** – audio pull quotes from artisans, champions, and longtime owners describing pivotal memories (first fitting, Grand American wins, father-to-child gun handoffs).
- **Heritage Events Feed** – list of traveling exhibitions, museum loans, Perazzi Invitational recaps.
